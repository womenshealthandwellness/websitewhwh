University (UTM) Resources
  • UTM Health & Counselling Centre

    Offers personal counselling, group counselling and psychiatric care to assist students experiencing a wide range of challenges. Additionally, there is a Tri-Campus Indigenous Wellness Counsellor and Coordinator available to support Indigenous students at U of T.

  • Accessibility Services

    Offers services and academic accommodations to students who have a documented learning, physical, sensory, mental health disability or medical condition.

  • U of T Telus Health Student Support (formerly known as My Student Support Program or MySSP)

    U of T Telus Health Student Support (THSS) offers immediate and confidential access to culturally relevant counselling in your native language, day or night. Counsellor is available via an app or by mobile phone, online chat, video, email or in person.

  • Sexual Violence Prevention & Support Centre (Tri-campus)

    Provides confidential, non-judgemental, client-centered services and support for community members who have been affected by sexual violence or harassment.

  • UTM's Campus Safety

    The University of Toronto Mississauga Campus Safety Special Constable Service is dedicated to creating a safe, secure, and equitable environment for students, staff and visitors.

UTMSU Resources
  • UTMSU Food Center Linktree

    Access all the key resources related to the UTMSU Food Center in one convenient location. The Linktree offers quick links to menus, order forms, and additional support services, making it easy to navigate and connect with everything you need.

General Community Support
  • Greater Toronto Area - General Mental Health Resources

    This service provides a range of accessible mental health supports for the Greater Toronto Area community. It includes counseling, crisis intervention, and referral services designed to help individuals manage mental health challenges effectively, ensuring timely and professional assistance for everyone in need.

  • Greater Toronto Area - Culturally Specific Mental Health Resources

    Tailored to address the unique needs of diverse communities, these resources offer culturally informed mental health support. By respecting different cultural identities, traditions, and languages, UTM Health and Counselling Centre ensures that individuals receive personalized care that resonates with their background, fostering a more inclusive and effective approach to mental well-being.

    Live Web Chat that offers 24/7 immediate help to all Indigenous people across Canada via phone or chat.
